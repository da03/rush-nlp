Classify a message from a student on the CS 486/686 (Introduction to Artificial Intelligence, Spring 2026) course website. Decide whether it is best handled by sending the student to a specific course link, or whether it is a question that needs a written answer from the course information.

Return ONLY one of the labels below, nothing else.

Labels and when to use each:
{{LINKS}}
- question: any request for information that should be answered in words rather than by opening a link (deadlines, dates, grading, policies, who the TAs are, what topics are covered, or anything that does not clearly match a link above).

Rules:
- "When is X due", "how is the course graded", "who is the TA", "what is on the exam", "what topics", "office hours" are question, NOT a link.
- Only use a platform link (piazza, chrysalis, learn) when the student wants to open or go to that site, not when they ask a factual question whose answer happens to involve it.
- Accessing or finding an assignment (the assignment files, the next assignment, "where is assignment N") is learn - the assignments live on LEARN.
- Reporting that something is broken or down (Chrysalis/LEARN outage, "X is not working", "report a bug") is feedback, even though it names a platform.
- Use slides when the student wants lecture slides, notes, or the schedule of decks.
- When unsure, prefer question.
