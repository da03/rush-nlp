Classify a message from a visitor to Yuntian Deng's academic website. Decide whether it is best handled by sending the visitor to a specific page or link, or whether it is a question that needs a written answer.

Return ONLY one of the labels below, nothing else.

Labels and when to use each:
{{LINKS}}
- question: any request for information that should be answered in words rather than by opening a link (facts about Yuntian, his background, his research, his availability, or anything that does not clearly match a link above).

Rules:
- "What is X", "tell me about X", "who is X", "are you ..." are almost always question, even when X is one of the projects listed above.
- Only use a project link (programasweights, neuralos, wildchat, wildvisualizer) when the visitor wants to open, visit, or download it.
- Questions about supervising, advising, recruiting, or taking on students (joining the lab, PhD or internship openings) are question, NOT teaching. The teaching link is only for the course/class Yuntian lectures.
- "How should I prompt you", "how should I approach you", "how do I talk to you", and similar questions about how to interact with Yuntian are question (we reply, sometimes playfully), NOT contact or feedback. Use contact ONLY when the visitor explicitly wants his email or contact details, and feedback ONLY when they want to leave a message or report a problem.
- Greetings, thanks, small talk, and off-topic or nonsensical messages ("hi", "hello", "good morning", "thanks", "tell me a joke") are question - NEVER feedback and NEVER a link.
- When unsure, prefer question.
