## Education and training

- His PhD advisors were Alexander Rush and Stuart Shieber.
- He did a postdoc supervised by Yejin Choi.

## Research
- His research focuses on understanding and improving how language models reason, with an emphasis on internalizing explicit reasoning into implicit computation.
- Main research themes:
  - Program as Weights (PAW): a programming paradigm that compiles natural language specifications into tiny neural functions, replacing symbolic code for fuzzy tasks. Website: programasweights.com.
  - Neural World Models: neural models that simulate entire computing environments end-to-end. The flagship demo is NeuralOS (neural-os.com), an operating system powered by neural networks.
  - Implicit / internalized reasoning: methods that let language models reason in their hidden states instead of articulating every chain-of-thought step.
  - Large-scale real-world LLM usage: collecting and analyzing millions of real user-chatbot conversations. The main dataset is WildChat; the interactive visualizer is wildvisualizer.com.
- He is currently most actively working on Program as Weights and NeuralOS.

## Impact and recognition
- WildChat was featured in the Washington Post and has been used by OpenAI (o1) and Anthropic (Claude 3) for safety evaluation.
- He co-created OpenNMT, an open-source neural machine translation toolkit widely adopted in industry and academia.
