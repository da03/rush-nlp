Causal masking makes training legal

Position \(t\) predicts token \(t+1\).

It may only see tokens \(\leq t\).

No future peeking, even during training.

Autoregressive factorization

A text probability is built one next-token prediction at a time:

\(P(x_1,\ldots,x_T)=\prod_{t=1}^{T}P(x_t\mid x_{<t})\)

Same idea as generation: left to right, token by token.

Cross-entropy for the next token

If the true next token is empty:

\(\text{loss} = -\log P(\text{empty}\mid\text{context})\)

This is multiclass classification over the vocabulary.

The loss, token by tokenlive model

Each bar is \(-\log p(\text{actual next token})\) — one sentence, many signals.

Interactive on the live slides: for a sentence, the model's cross-entropy loss at each position is \(-\log p\) of the token that actually came next. Predictable tokens (like "up" after "picked") score low; surprising tokens score high. Summing these is exactly the pretraining objective, and one sentence supplies a loss at every position.

The pretraining loop

ids = tokenizer(text)
x = ids[:-1]
y = ids[1:]

logits = model(x)              # [T, vocab_size]
loss = cross_entropy(logits, y)
loss.backward()
optimizer.step()

This is L17's training loop, scaled to billions/trillions of tokens.

Sampling and temperature

Temperature reshapes the distribution:

\(\operatorname{softmax}(\text{logits}/T)\)

low \(T\)

sharper, safer, repetitive

high \(T\)

flatter, diverse, risky

Temperature, hands-onlive

Low temperature sharpens onto one token; high temperature flattens toward uniform.

Interactive on the live slides: drag the temperature and the scores and watch softmax turn them into a probability distribution. Low temperature concentrates probability on the top token (safe, repetitive); high temperature spreads it out (diverse, risky).

Restrict randomness to plausible tokens

top-k

sample from the \(k\) most likely tokens

top-p

sample from the smallest set with total probability \(\geq p\)

Decoding choices shape output even when weights are fixed.
